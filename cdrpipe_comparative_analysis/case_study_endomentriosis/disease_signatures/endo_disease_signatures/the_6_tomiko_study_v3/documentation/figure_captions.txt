FIGURE 1: Drug Discovery Overlap Analysis
-----------------------------------------
File: figure1_overlap_analysis.png / figure1_overlap_analysis.pdf

Caption:
Overlap analysis of drug candidates identified across three methodological 
approaches in the Tomiko endometriosis study. Bar heights represent the number 
of unique drugs in each overlap category. The NEW CMAP method demonstrates 
substantially higher drug discovery capacity (n=932 unique drugs, 67.7% of 
total) compared to NEW TAHOE (n=22 unique drugs, 1.6%) and OLD studies (n=185 
unique drugs, 13.4%). A total of 62 drugs were found by all three methods, 
representing the highest-confidence candidates for endometriosis treatment. 
Colors: CMAP (Orange), TAHOE (Blue), OLD (Green), overlaps (Purple/Teal).

FIGURE 2: Method Comparison Summary
-----------------------------------
File: figure2_method_comparison.png / figure2_method_comparison.pdf

Caption:
Three-panel summary comparing drug repurposing methods for endometriosis. 
(A) Total unique drugs identified: NEW CMAP (n=1,307), NEW TAHOE (n=85), OLD 
studies (n=323). CMAP identifies approximately 15-fold more drugs than TAHOE. 
(B) Method-specific discoveries: Drugs found exclusively by each approach, 
demonstrating CMAP's dominance in novel candidate identification. (C) 
Distribution of drug discoveries across methods, showing that 67.7% of all 
unique drugs (932/1,377) were identified only by the CMAP methodology. This 
pattern contrasts sharply with autoimmune diseases where TAHOE outperforms CMAP, 
suggesting disease-specific optimal method selection.

FIGURE 3: Experiment-Level Comparison
-------------------------------------
File: figure3_experiment_comparison.png / figure3_experiment_comparison.pdf

Caption:
Experiment-level comparison of CMAP and TAHOE performance across 6 endometriosis 
experiments. (A) Side-by-side comparison of drug hits per experiment shows 
consistent CMAP superiority across all experimental conditions. (B) TAHOE/CMAP 
ratio analysis reveals that TAHOE identifies only 3-6% of the drugs found by 
CMAP in each experiment (ratios ranging from 0.028 to 0.064). Red dashed line 
indicates 5% threshold. The esesamples experiment yielded zero hits from both 
methods. This consistent pattern across independent experiments strengthens the 
conclusion that CMAP is the preferred methodology for endometriosis drug 
repurposing.

----------------------------------
File: figure4_summary_table.png / figure4_summary_table.pdf

Caption:
Summary statistics for the Tomiko endometriosis drug repurposing validation 
study. Key findings: (1) CMAP identified 1,307 total unique drugs vs TAHOE's 85

KEY NARRATIVE FOR MANUSCRIPT:

The Tomiko endometriosis study reveals a striking methodological insight: CMAP 
dramatically outperforms TAHOE in this disease context, with approximately 15× 
more drug candidates identified. This contrasts with our autoimmune disease 
analysis where TAHOE significantly outperformed CMAP (76.6% vs 17.8% recovery).

This disease-specific performance pattern suggests:
1. No single connectivity mapping method is universally optimal
2. Disease pathophysiology influences method effectiveness
3. Hormonal/reproductive diseases (endometriosis) may favor CMAP approaches
4. Immune-mediated diseases may favor TAHOE approaches

The high recovery rate of OLD study findings by CMAP (79.3%) validates both 
the original studies and the CMAP methodology for this disease type.
