FIGURE CAPTIONS FOR AUTOIMMUNE DISEASE DRUG REPURPOSING STUDY
==============================================================

FIGURE 1: Comprehensive Analysis of CMAP and TAHOE Drug Repurposing Performance
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Comparative analysis of two computational drug repurposing methods across 20 autoimmune diseases. 
(A) Grouped bar chart comparing known drug recovery rates between CMAP (blue) and TAHOE (red) for 
each disease, with dashed lines indicating method averages. (B) Paired slope chart where each line 
represents one disease, illustrating the change in recovery rate from CMAP to TAHOE (green lines 
indicate TAHOE superiority; red lines indicate CMAP superiority). (C) Complementarity analysis 
showing the distribution of recovered known drugs: CMAP-only (blue), both methods (purple), 
and TAHOE-only (red), demonstrating minimal overlap between approaches. (D) Box plot comparison 
of recovery rate distributions with individual disease data points overlaid 
(Wilcoxon signed-rank test p = 0.0002). (E) Scatter plot showing the relationship between 
total drug hits and known drug recovery rate for both methods. (F) Horizontal bar chart of 
the top 10 diseases ranked by total unique drug repurposing candidates.


FIGURE 2: Recovery Rate Heatmap Comparing CMAP and TAHOE Across Autoimmune Diseases
------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Heatmap visualization of known drug recovery rates (%) for CMAP and TAHOE methods across 20 
autoimmune diseases. Diseases are ordered by TAHOE recovery rate (highest to lowest). 
Color intensity represents recovery rate from 0% (red) to 100% (green). Values are annotated 
within each cell. TAHOE demonstrates consistently higher recovery rates across the majority 
of diseases, with 9 diseases achieving 100% recovery. Notable exceptions include type 1 diabetes 
mellitus and arthritis, where CMAP showed superior performance.


FIGURE 3: Summary of Drug Repurposing Method Comparison in Autoimmune Diseases
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Publication-ready summary of key findings. (A) Box and strip plot comparing known drug 
recovery rates between CMAP and TAHOE, demonstrating significantly higher performance for 
TAHOE (Wilcoxon p < 0.001, Cohen's d = 2.35, large effect size). Individual disease data 
points are shown with jitter for visualization. (B) Stacked horizontal bar showing the 
complementarity of the two methods: of 170 total known drugs recovered, 54 (31.8%) were 
identified exclusively by CMAP, 110 (64.7%) exclusively by TAHOE, and only 6 (3.5%) by both methods. 
(C) Diseases achieving 100% recovery rate with TAHOE (9 of 20 diseases), ordered by the number of 
known drugs available in the TAHOE database. (D) Summary statistics table comparing key performance 
metrics between CMAP and TAHOE methods.


FIGURE 4: Disease-Specific Performance Profiles for Selected Autoimmune Conditions
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Radar charts depicting multi-dimensional performance profiles for six representative autoimmune 
diseases: Sjögren's syndrome, rheumatoid arthritis, type 1 diabetes mellitus, systemic lupus 
erythematosus, multiple sclerosis, and Crohn's disease. Each chart compares CMAP (blue) and 
TAHOE (red) across four normalized metrics: recovery rate, number of drug hits, known drugs 
available in the database, and total database size. These profiles reveal disease-specific 
patterns where TAHOE generally demonstrates broader coverage, with the exception of type 1 diabetes 
mellitus where CMAP shows superior recovery rate performance. Percentage values in subtitles indicate 
the actual recovery rates for each method.

SUPPLEMENTARY INFORMATION
==============================================================

Statistical Methods:
- Wilcoxon signed-rank test used for paired non-parametric comparison
- Cohen's d calculated for effect size estimation
- All recovery rates calculated as: (drugs recovered / known drugs available) × 100

- CMAP: Connectivity Map
- TAHOE: [Full name of TAHOE method]
- MS: Multiple Sclerosis

Data Source:
- 20 autoimmune diseases analyzed
- Known drug validation against clinical trial and approved drug databases
