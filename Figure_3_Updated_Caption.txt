Fig. 3. Precision and Recall Performance Across Diseases. (A) Recall distribution density showing the probability density of recall values for CMap (orange, n=165 diseases) and Tahoe-100M (blue, n=156 diseases). Dashed vertical lines indicate the mean recall for each platform (CMap: 18.5%, Tahoe-100M: 47.3%), illustrating Tahoe-100M's significantly superior recovery of known drug-disease relationships. Sample sizes reflect diseases with at least one known therapeutic drug available in each database (recall calculation requires P > 0). (B) Precision distribution density displaying right-skewed profiles for both platforms, with the majority of diseases achieving low precision due to the inherent challenge of predicting validated relationships from transcriptomic signatures alone. Mean precision values are indicated: CMap 1.3% (median 0.0%) and Tahoe-100M 1.8% (median 0.3%), measured across all diseases with at least one prediction (I > 0). (C) Scatter plot of Precision versus Recall, where each point represents a single disease (CMap n=208, Tahoe-100M n=223). Dotted reference lines at 50% highlight high-performance quadrants. Sample sizes in panel C reflect diseases with at least one prediction made (precision calculation requires I > 0); these differ from panel A due to platform-specific prediction coverage. Tahoe-100M (blue) clusters at substantially higher recall values compared to CMap (orange) while maintaining comparable precision, demonstrating superior overall recovery of therapeutically validated drug-disease relationships. Note: The analysis began with 234 diseases; variable sample sizes reflect the filtering requirements for each metric (P > 0 for recall, I > 0 for precision), as some diseases lack drug predictions or known therapeutic relationships in the respective databases.
