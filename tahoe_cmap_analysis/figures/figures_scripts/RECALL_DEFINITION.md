# Recall Calculation and Interpretation

## Definition and Calculation

**Recall** measures the fraction of known drug-disease associations that were successfully recovered by each pipeline, calculated as: **Recall = (Known drugs identified by pipeline / Total known drugs available for that disease) × 100%**. A recall of 50% means the pipeline found half of the known therapeutic drugs for a given disease, making it a direct measure of the pipeline's sensitivity and comprehensiveness in recovering established treatments.

## What It Means

High recall indicates that the pipeline is effective at identifying most of the known drugs associated with a disease (good sensitivity), making it valuable for comprehensive drug discovery screens where missing known therapeutics would be a significant loss. Low recall suggests the pipeline filters conservatively or has limited coverage, meaning many known drugs for that disease are not recovered—useful when confidence in predictions is more important than exhaustive coverage, but problematic if completeness is the goal.
