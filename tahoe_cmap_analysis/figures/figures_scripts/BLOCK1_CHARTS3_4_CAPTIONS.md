## Captions for Block 1 Charts 3 & 4

### Figure 3: Signature Strength Distribution

**Figure 3. Experimental Signature Strength Across Datasets.** Signature strength represents the mean absolute fold change in gene expression for each drug experiment, quantifying the magnitude of biological perturbation. TAHOE exhibits a concentrated distribution with median strength of approximately 0.58–0.82, indicating consistently strong, detectable drug effects across its experimental library. In contrast, CMap shows a bimodal distribution with peaks near 0.42 and 0.78, reflecting a mixture of high quality and lower quality experiments within its dataset. The rightward shift of TAHOE's distribution suggests its experiments produce clearer transcriptomic signatures, providing stronger input signals for downstream drug-disease connectivity analysis. This superior baseline signal quality contributes to TAHOE's ability to detect subtle drug-disease associations and achieve higher recall in drug repurposing predictions.

### Figure 4: Signature Stability Across Experimental Conditions

**Figure 4. Reproducibility of Drug Signatures Across Conditions in TAHOE.** This analysis evaluates the stability of TAHOE drug signatures by computing Pearson correlations between signatures measured under different experimental conditions: varying drug doses and different cell lines. Dose consistency (measuring the same drug at multiple concentrations) shows a primary peak at correlation approximately 0.70, indicating that drug-induced transcriptomic changes are robust across concentration ranges. Cell line consistency (measuring across different cellular contexts) peaks at approximately 0.60, showing good but somewhat greater variability reflecting inherent differences in cell line transcriptomes. A small population of drugs (5–10%) exhibits low correlations (<0.35), indicating condition-sensitive effects. The overall strong correlation distribution (median >0.60 for both measures) demonstrates that TAHOE signatures are reproducible and generalizable across experimental contexts, providing confidence that predicted drug-disease connections will be relevant across diverse patient populations and treatment scenarios.
