This analysis performed a large-scale comparative evaluation of therapeutic candidates 
across two major drug signature databases (CMAP and TAHOE) using 233 manually curated 
disease signatures from CREEDS. The analysis identified 6,161+ drug-disease associations 
and validated 33 high-confidence drugs through both methods.

Key Metrics:
• Total Disease Signatures: 233 (CREEDS manually curated)
• Drug Signature Databases: CMAP and TAHOE
• Total Drug-Disease Associations: 6,161+
• Shared Drugs (CMAP & TAHOE): 61 drugs
• High-Confidence Candidates (Validated by Both): 33 drugs
• Q-value Threshold: 0.5 (primary analysis)

Core Parameters:

Method Description (Reverse Scoring Enabled):
- For each disease signature: matches UP genes to drug signatures
- For each disease signature: matches DOWN genes to drug signatures
- Scores both CMAP and TAHOE separately
- Enables discovery of both direct (same direction) and inverse relationships


2.2 DATABASE COMPARISON: TAHOE vs CMAP
──────────────────────────────────────

Comprehensive Feature Comparison:

EXPERIMENTAL SCALE
• TAHOE: 56,827 experiments (56 experiments per drug on average)
• CMAP: 6,100 experiments (varied replicates per drug: 1-182)
• Insight: TAHOE provides more balanced replicates; CMAP offers broader coverage

DRUG COVERAGE
• TAHOE: 379 unique drugs (136-150 replicates per compound)
• CMAP: 1,309 unique drugs (1-182 replicates per compound)
• Shared Drugs: 85 compounds appear in both databases
• Strategic Value: CMAP > 3× more unique compounds for drug repurposing discovery

CELL LINE DIVERSITY
• TAHOE: 50 unique cell lines (broader diversity)
• CMAP: 5 canonical cell lines (e.g., MCF7)
• Advantage: TAHOE captures heterogeneous cellular responses

CONCENTRATION / DOSAGE TRACKING
• TAHOE: 56,827 unique concentrations (0.05 – 5 µM) per-experiment precision
• CMAP: 156 unique concentrations (0.00001 – 10 µM range)
• Advantage: CMAP covers wider dose range; TAHOE stores precise per-experiment values

EXPOSURE DURATION
• TAHOE: Not explicitly specified
• CMAP: 2 explicit durations (6 h and 24 h typical)
• Advantage: CMAP enables temporal response analysis

GENE COVERAGE & TECHNOLOGY
• TAHOE: 62,710 genes (full transcriptome via single-cell RNA-seq, aggregated)
• CMAP: 12,000-22,000 genes (varies by platform: Microarray/L1000)
• Technology: TAHOE = single-cell resolution; CMAP = bulk profiling
• Advantage: TAHOE captures cell-specific effects; CMAP yields smooth, stable profiles

DATA MEASUREMENTS
• TAHOE: Log₂FC, p-values, adjusted p-values, base mean (4 matrices per experiment)
• CMAP: Pre-computed rank order lists (gene positions per drug)
• Advantage: TAHOE stores raw statistics; CMAP provides normalized rankings

QUALITY CONTROLS & METADATA
• TAHOE: Batch/Plate IDs (14 plates), assumed DMSO vehicle
• CMAP: Explicit vehicle tracking (3 unique vehicles, DMSO = 6,040), 26 drug vendors, CAS/DrugBank IDs
• Advantage: CMAP enables better chemical tracking; TAHOE enables batch correction

REPLICATES & TECHNICAL CONTROLS
• TAHOE: Single-cell counts tracked (n_cells_trt: 1-16,414; n_cells_ctrl: 13-15,253)
• CMAP: Bulk aggregated (no single-cell tracking)
• Advantage: TAHOE enables statistical power analysis per single-cell experiment

FILE ORGANIZATION & SIZE
• TAHOE: aggregated.h5 (~26 GB, 56,827 experiments × 62,710 genes = 3.57B values)
• CMAP: CSV + RData (~600 KB, more compact due to pre-ranking)
• Data Depth: TAHOE provides complete expression matrices; CMAP provides ranked genes

COMPLEMENTARY STRENGTHS

The dual-database approach captures orthogonal biological information:

TAHOE Advantages:
✓ Higher gene resolution (full transcriptome)

✓ Explicit exposure times (6 h, 24 h)
✓ Vendor metadata for chemical sourcing
✓ Standardized platform (L1000)

ANALYSIS IMPLICATION:
By integrating both databases, this analysis:
1. Achieves broad drug coverage (CMAP) + deep mechanistic insights (TAHOE)
2. Validates predictions through cross-database consensus
3. Identifies robust drug-disease associations through method triangulation
