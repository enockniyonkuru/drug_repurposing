# Block 2 Alternative Signature Strength Visualizations

## Figure 6B Option 1: Violin Plot with Jittered Disease Points

Disease signatures show comparable strength between up and down-regulated genes. Up-regulated genes exhibited a median strength of 0.026 log2 fold change (mean=0.025, SD=0.006), while down-regulated genes had a median strength of 0.026 log2 fold change (mean=0.026, SD=0.010). The violin plot reveals the full distribution of signature strength across all 233 diseases, with individual disease points overlaid to show the heterogeneity in regulation patterns. The boxplot overlay indicates that both gene regulation directions maintain similar quartile distributions, suggesting that diseases tend to have comparable effect sizes regardless of whether genes are up or down-regulated.

## Figure 6B Option 2: Up vs Down Regulated Gene Strength Per Disease

Disease signatures vary in their regulation balance, with distinct patterns observed across the 233 diseases analyzed. When comparing up-regulated gene strength (X-axis) to down-regulated gene strength (Y-axis), approximately 27% of diseases exhibit strong up-regulation (red, >1.3× more up-regulated genes), 25% show strong down-regulation (blue, >1.3× more down-regulated genes), and 47% maintain balanced regulation patterns (gray). The dashed diagonal line indicates perfect balance; diseases above this line have stronger up-regulation, while those below show stronger down-regulation. Bubble size represents the total number of genes in each disease signature, revealing that disease signatures span a wide range of regulatory compositions despite similar overall effect sizes.
