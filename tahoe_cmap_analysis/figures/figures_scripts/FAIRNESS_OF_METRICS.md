## Why These Are Fair Success Metrics

These four complementary metrics together establish a fair, multidimensional assessment of pipeline performance that accounts for inherent differences in design and avoids systematic bias toward either approach. Enrichment factor normalizes for baseline expectations in each dataset, revealing genuine predictive signal independent of pool size—both pipelines are evaluated on whether they outperform random selection by comparable margins. Success at top N depth curves use identical ranking thresholds for both methods, measuring only ranking quality without penalizing CMAP for returning fewer candidates; this puts both on equal footing regardless of search breadth. Normalized success per disease converts raw recovery counts into fractions (0–1 scale) so that finding 5 drugs from a 5-drug disease registers identically to finding 5 from a 20-drug disease, eliminating bias from disease complexity heterogeneity. Finally, Jaccard similarity evaluates overlap on a symmetric disease-by-disease basis, where neither pipeline receives advantage from candidate pool size—the metric purely captures whether predictions converge or diverge. Collectively, these metrics answer distinct, clinically relevant questions (Does it beat random? How quickly? How comprehensively? How redundant with alternatives?) while controlling for confounders that could inflate one pipeline's apparent advantage, ensuring that observed differences reflect true algorithmic superiority rather than methodological artifacts.
