## Captions for Block 2 Charts 6 & 6B

### Figure 6: Total Disease Signature Size Before and After Filtering

**Figure 6. Disease Signature Size Distribution with Multi-Stage Quality Control Filtering.** Disease signatures underwent two-stage quality control filtering to ensure robust, biologically coherent signals. During standardization preprocessing, genes were filtered by mean-median consistency (requiring that mean and median log2 fold change have the same sign) and median effect size (absolute median log2FC must be at least 0.02). During downstream analysis, genes were further filtered to require absolute log2 fold change greater than 1.0 with statistical significance (adjusted p value <0.05). This multi-stage filtering approach removed approximately 12% of genes per disease on average overall (from mean 974.7 genes before downstream filtering to mean 858.2 genes after downstream filtering), eliminating low-confidence and inconsistent signals while preserving the core biological signal. The box plot shows the distribution of gene counts across 233 diseases before and after the final filtering step. These stringent criteria ensure that downstream drug-disease connectivity predictions are based on validated differential expression patterns with both directional consistency and substantial effect sizes.

### Figure 6B: Disease Signature Strength for Up and Down Regulated Genes

**Figure 6B. Strength Comparison of Up and Down Regulated Disease Signatures.** Disease signature strength is quantified as the mean absolute log2 fold change for genes in each direction of regulation, providing a measure of the magnitude of transcriptomic perturbation associated with each disease. Up-regulated genes (red) and down-regulated genes (blue) show nearly identical strength distributions across the 233 disease signatures, with median log2 fold changes of approximately 0.026 (mean of ~0.5 across all genes within each signature), indicating comparable biological impact from both gene expression directions. The overlapping distributions suggest that disease-induced gene expression changes are symmetric in magnitude between upregulation and downregulation. The concentration of strength values at moderate levels reflects that most differential genes show modest fold changes, while the tail extending toward higher strengths represents genes with more dramatic expression changes that contribute disproportionately to disease phenotypes.
