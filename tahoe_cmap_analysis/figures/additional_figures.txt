BLOCK 1 — Drug Signature Charts (CMap and Tahoe)
These charts satisfy all your PI feedback about describing the datasets and showing filtering effects.
 
Chart 1. Experiment Count Before and After Filtering
What to include:
• CMap: number of experiments before QC and after QC
• Tahoe: number of experiments before QC and after QC
• Use two bars for each dataset: before vs after
How to make it:
In R or Python count rows in signature matrices before and after applying your internal consistency threshold.
Plot a bar chart with two groups (CMap, Tahoe).
 
Chart 2. Gene Universe Before and After Filtering
What to include:
• Number of genes available in CMap before mapping
• Number after mapping to shared universe
• Same for Tahoe
How to make it:
Count genes in raw matrices, then count genes in final matrices after mapping.
Plot as grouped bars per dataset.
 
Chart 3. Signature Strength Distribution
What to include:
• For each experiment compute one metric:
absolute mean fold change or number of genes with absolute fold change greater than 1
• Plot distribution for CMap
• Plot distribution for Tahoe
Use box plots or density curves.
How to make it:
Extract fold change values across all experiments for each dataset.
Compute a strength measure per experiment and plot.
 
Chart 4. Signature Stability Across Conditions (Tahoe only)
What to include:
• Dose consistency: correlation of signatures across doses for each drug
• Cell line consistency: correlation of signatures across cell lines for each drug
• Distribution of these correlations
How to make it:
Group Tahoe signatures by drug ID.
Within each group compute pairwise Pearson correlations.
Plot density of correlation values.

BLOCK 4 — Success Metric Charts (Beyond Precision and Recall)
These measure success in a fair, normalized way.
 
Chart 11. Enrichment Factor Distribution
What to include:
• Compute enrichment for each disease for both platforms
• Plot distribution for CMap and Tahoe side by side
How to make it:
Formula:
observed precision divided by expected precision
expected precision equals known drugs in universe divided by total candidate drugs
Compute per disease and plot density.
 
Chart 12. Success at Top N Depth Curves
What to include:
• X axis: depth from 1 to maybe 200
• Y axis: fraction of diseases with at least one known drug in the top X hits
• Two lines: CMap and Tahoe
How to make it:
For each disease generate ranking of drugs.
At each depth compute whether a known drug appears.
Aggregate across diseases and plot curves.
 
Chart 13. Normalized Success per Disease
What to include:
• For each disease:
number of known drugs recovered divided by total known drugs available
• Plot two distributions: CMap and Tahoe
How to make it:
Compute per disease fractional recall normalized by known drug count.
Plot distribution.
 
Chart 14. Jaccard Similarity per Disease
What to include:
• For each disease compute Jaccard on the sets of top hits:
intersection divided by union
• Plot distribution of these similarity values
How to make it:
Take top N hits for Tahoe and CMap.
Compute Jaccard and plot.
 
Chart 15. Global Venn Diagram of All Hits
What to include:
• Set of all significant hits across all diseases in Tahoe
• Set of all in CMap
• Draw Venn diagram for overlap
How to make it:
Collect all unique hits from each pipeline.
Use any Venn diagram function.

For each disease:
• Volcano plot of fold change vs significance
• Number of up genes and down genes

Use the differential expression table for that disease.
Plot log fold change on x axis and negative log p value on y axis.
Color up genes and down genes.
 
CASE STUDY CHART 2 — Top Hits From Tahoe and CMap
What to include:
• Bar plot of top ten Tahoe hits
• Bar plot of top ten CMap hits
• Highlight known drugs in a special color
• Use the same colors for the pipelines as everywhere else
How to make it:
Extract the ranked drug tables for that disease from each platform.
Plot bars of connectivity score or negative q value.

• Hits from CMap for that disease

• Label known drugs showing where they fall

Take top N hits from Tahoe and CMap.
Generate a Venn diagram.
