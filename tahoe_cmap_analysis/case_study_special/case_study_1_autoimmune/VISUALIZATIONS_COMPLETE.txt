## 📝 Figure Captions (Ready to Use)

### For `03_all_diseases_recall.png`
> "Figure X: Recall performance of TAHOE and CMAP methods across four disease cases. Recall represents the percentage of known disease-drug pairs (from OpenTargets database) successfully recovered by each computational approach. Results demonstrate variable recovery rates across diseases, with CMAP showing higher recall for thrombocytopenic purpura (89%) while TAHOE shows more consistent performance across other diseases."

### For `02_all_diseases_hit_distribution.png`
> "Figure X: Comparative analysis of drug candidate identification using TAHOE and CMAP methods. Total hit counts vary substantially between methods and diseases, with CMAP generally identifying larger candidate sets while TAHOE provides more conservative predictions."

### For `06_performance_scatter.png`
> "Figure X: Method complementarity analysis showing TAHOE and CMAP recall performance. Each bubble represents one disease (size proportional to total hits identified). The diagonal dashed line indicates equal performance. Points above the line indicate superior CMAP recall; below indicates superior TAHOE recall."
